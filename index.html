Course Description

Trigonometry includes study of the six trigonometric functions and their inverse functions; solve right and oblique triangles; know and apply basic identities and simplify trigonometric expressions using identities; solve trigonometric equations; graphing trigonometric functions; understand and apply De Moivre's theorem and the nth-root theorem; understand and use complex numbers and polar coordinates; solve application problems that rely on trigonometry.

  • Grades

    Final grades calculated from the following:
    • 70%   - 8 Chapter Quizzes (lowest grade dropped)
    • 15%   - 1 Final Exam
    • 15%    - Homework (submitted in MyMathLab)

  • Missed Exames

    One missed exam may be made-up during the course for any reason. Makeup exams must be scheduled within one week of the original exam date. Any exam that is missed and not made up will be scored as a zero. In the absence of a documented disability, exams must be completed in the time allotted.

  • Attendance

    Any student who is recorded as absent for more than 25% of scheduled classes, will have his/her grade reduced by one full letter grade. A student who is recorded as absent for more that 40% of scheduled classes or who misses the final exam will be given a grade of F1-failure based on non-completion of the course.

  • Withdrawl

    As per MATC policy, any student who wishes to do so may withdraw prior to the completion of 90% of the course. The instructor has no power to withdraw a student from the course. (See http://matcmadison.edu/program-and-coursewithdrawals)

  • Academic Integrity

    Policies regarding cheating and other misconduct will be strictly adhered to. It is your responsibility to review these policies on the MATC website: http://matcmadison.edu/academic-integrity.
